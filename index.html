HOW TO READ PATTERNS
EXAMPLE: SPREAD PATTERN
Pattern: "Line: Dog (+3.5 to +7.5) | Move: Big move toward | Tickets: 30-45% | Money: 65-75%"
Means: When a team getting 3.5 to 7.5 points has only 30-45% of public tickets but 65-75% of the money is on them AND the line moved toward them significantly → sharp money is pouring in on the underdog while public bets the favorite.
EV: If this shows +14%, betting that underdog has historically returned +14% per dollar wagered.
EXAMPLE: MONEYLINE PATTERN
Pattern: "Line: Slight dog (+110 to +200) | Move: Move toward (+2 to +5pts) | Tickets: 45-55% | Money: 55-65%"
Means: A slight underdog priced +110 to +200 where the line moved in their favor (they got cheaper to bet) while money split slightly toward them.
EV: Positive EV here = the market is undervaluing this team relative to where sharp money is pointing.
TICKET% VS MONEY%
Key insight: Ticket % = how many bettors. Money % = how much dollar value.
When money% >> ticket% → a few large bets (sharp money) → follow the money.
When ticket% >> money% → many small bets (public squares) → consider fading.
The pattern table shows you which combinations of these splits have historically produced positive EV.

LINE TRACKER MODEL — v1.0
A machine learning system that finds edges over sportsbooks by analyzing how betting lines move from open to close.
The model tracks every line snapshot across 9 books — 6 public, 3 sharp — every 30 minutes. It learns which movement patterns historically predict when a line is mispriced relative to its true probability, then sizes bets using fractional Kelly Criterion.
⚙️ Model Methodology
1
Data Collection
The scraper pulls live odds from The Odds API every 30 minutes across 9 books and 6 sports. Each run creates a new price snapshot appended to the event's line history file.
2
Feature Engineering
30+ movement features are extracted per event: how much Pinnacle moved, how fast, whether public books followed, sharp vs public divergence, reverse line movement flags, and splits data when available.
3
XGBoost Classification
A separate XGBoost model is trained per sport per market (e.g. NBA Moneyline, MLB Spreads). It predicts P(bet wins) from movement features. Platt scaling calibrates the output probabilities.
4
Edge Detection
Edge = Model probability − Pinnacle no-vig fair probability. If the model believes a team has a 55% chance of winning but Pinnacle's fair price implies 50%, that's a +5% edge.
5
Kelly Criterion Sizing
Bets are sized at 25% of full Kelly to account for model uncertainty. Hard caps: max 4% per bet, max 20% total exposure. Portfolio-level scaling prevents over-concentration.
🎯 What the Model Predicts

The model predicts the probability that a bet wins based on how the line has moved — not who wins the game outright. It is purely a market inefficiency detector.

📊 Key Indicators (Ranked)
#1
pin_move_6h HIGH
Pinnacle's implied probability change in the last 6 hours. Late sharp steam is the single strongest predictor of a mispriced line.
#2
sharp_pub_divergence HIGH
American odds gap between the best public book and the best sharp book. Large gaps indicate sharp money has moved the sharp price but public books haven't caught up.
#3
sig_rlm HIGH
Reverse Line Movement flag. Triggers when 65%+ of public tickets are on one side but the line moves the other way — the clearest sign of sharp action.
#4
money_vs_tickets HIGH
Sharp money % minus ticket % from VSiN splits. A large positive value means big bets are on one side while small bettors are on the other — classic sharp signal.
#5
pin_move_speed MED
Rate of Pinnacle's probability change per hour. Fast moves indicate urgent sharp action; slow drift is less significant.
#6
cross_book_std_change MED
Change in standard deviation of implied probabilities across all books. Increasing disagreement = market inefficiency forming.
#7
hours_to_game MED
Time until game start. Edges found close to game time (under 6 hours) are weighted more heavily as lines are more settled and sharp.
#8
n_reversals LOW
Number of times the line changed direction. High reversals = contested market with two-way sharp action; lower confidence edge.
📖 How to Use the Slate
1
Focus on HIGH confidence bets first
HIGH confidence = edge ≥ 7% AND Kelly ≥ 2.5%. These are the model's strongest signals. MEDIUM = edge ≥ 4%. LOW = below that — smaller position sizes recommended.
2
Check the Signals column
SHARP = Pinnacle moved while public books stayed flat. RLM = public on one side, line moved other way. FADE = public overloaded one side with no sharp confirmation. CLV = pure model edge only.
3
Use the Bet$ column for sizing
The Bet$ amount is already Kelly-sized for a $10,000 bankroll. Scale proportionally for your bankroll: if you have $5,000, bet half the listed amount.
4
Bet at the listed book
The Book column shows where the best available price was found at scrape time. Check the current price before placing — lines move. If the price is worse than listed, recalculate edge.
5
Refresh before games start
The model updates every 30–60 minutes automatically. Hit ↺ REFRESH before placing any bet to ensure you have the latest slate. Edges can disappear as books adjust.
⚠️ Model Blindspots
✗
No Injury / News Awareness
The model does not know about late scratches, injuries, weather, or breaking news. A line moving because of a key player being ruled out looks identical to sharp money to the model. Always cross-check injury reports before betting.
✗
Early Season / Small Sample
Models trained on fewer than 100 real game outcomes are using synthetic data. AUC of 0.50 means the model is guessing. Treat early-season predictions with skepticism until AUC consistently exceeds 0.55.
✗
No VSiN Splits Data
VSiN splits (public betting % vs money %) are currently returning 0 results — the RLM and Public Fade signals are based solely on line movement, not actual ticket/money percentages. These signals will strengthen when splits data flows.
✗
Limited Historical Depth
Bootstrap pulls 3 days of history. The model's pattern recognition improves significantly after 30+ days of continuous scraping with real movement data across full game cycles.
✗
CFB Off-Season Games
College football spring games and early futures have very thin markets with low sharp action. These lines move for different reasons than regular season games. CFB predictions are least reliable outside Sept–Jan.
✗
Book Limits
The model does not account for the fact that sharp books (Pinnacle, Circa) may limit or ban winning bettors. Public books listed are the betting target but have lower limits.
🚀 How to Improve the Model
↑
Let it run for 30+ days
The single biggest improvement is time. More snapshots = better movement patterns. More completed games = real outcome labels instead of synthetic. AUC should climb from 0.50 toward 0.57–0.62 with a full season of data.
↑
Add an injury API
Integrate RotowWire or ESPN's injury feed to flag games with key player scratches. Add a binary "has_major_injury" feature — this would dramatically reduce false positives caused by injury-driven line moves.
↑
Fix VSiN splits scraping
The public betting % data from VSiN would unlock the RLM and Public Fade signals properly. Fix the parser or switch to Action Network's public API for splits data.
↑
Raise MIN_SAMPLES_TO_TRAIN
Once you have 100+ real outcomes per sport, change MIN_SAMPLES_TO_TRAIN back to 80 and MIN_REAL_OUTCOMES to 100 in configs/config.py. The model will retrain on real data and edges will be much more reliable.
↑
Add opening line tracking
The bootstrap history provides some opening lines but gaps exist. Storing the true market open (first posted line) separately from movement history would improve the line move magnitude features significantly.
↑
Raise MIN_EDGE_TO_BET gradually
Currently set to 0.5% to generate output during bootstrap. Once you have validated CLV > 0 in backtests, raise this to 2.5% (configs/config.py → MIN_EDGE_TO_BET = 0.025). This filters out low-conviction bets.
🗄️ Data Sources
📡
The Odds API
Primary odds source. Provides live and historical lines for ML, spreads, and totals across all 9 books. Also provides player prop pricing per event and completed game scores for outcome labeling. Updated every 30 minutes via scraper.
📊
VSiN (Vegas Stats & Information Network)
Source for public betting splits — ticket % vs money % per market. Also provides player prop historical hit rates vs current lines. Currently returning limited data; used when available to power RLM and Public Fade signals.
🏦
Sharp Books: Pinnacle, Circa, Bookmaker
Used as the sharp reference price. Pinnacle is the global standard for efficient pricing and is used as the CLV benchmark. Movement at these books vs public books is the core edge detection signal.
🏪
Public Books: DraftKings, FanDuel, BetMGM, Bovada, William Hill, Bet365
Target betting books. The model identifies when these books are slow to adjust to sharp movement, creating a window where positive EV bets are available at above-fair-value prices.
🤖
GitHub Actions (Automation)
All data collection, model training, and prediction generation runs automatically on GitHub's servers. Scraper fires every 30 minutes, training every 2 hours, predictions every hour. No local infrastructure required.
📋 Reading the Slate — Column Guide
Model%
The model's estimated win probability after calibration. This is the core output — how likely the model thinks this bet is to win.
Fair%
Pinnacle's no-vig implied probability — the market's best estimate of true probability after removing the bookmaker's margin.
Edge
Model% minus Fair%. The core metric. Positive edge = model believes this bet is underpriced. Higher is better. Anything above 5% is considered significant.
EV%
Expected value as a percentage of stake. EV% = (Model% × profit on win) − (1 − Model%). Positive EV = profitable bet in the long run.
Bet$
Kelly-sized bet amount for a $10,000 bankroll. Already scaled to 25% fractional Kelly with a 4% hard cap per bet and 20% total exposure cap.
Conf (Confidence)
HIGH = edge ≥ 7% and Kelly ≥ 2.5%. MEDIUM = edge ≥ 4% and Kelly ≥ 1.2%. LOW = below those thresholds. Prioritize HIGH and MEDIUM bets.
