HOW TO READ PATTERNS
EXAMPLE: SPREAD PATTERN
Pattern: "Line: Dog (+3.5 to +7.5) | Move: Big move toward | Tickets: 30-45% | Money: 65-75%"
Means: When a team getting 3.5 to 7.5 points has only 30-45% of public tickets but 65-75% of the money is on them AND the line moved toward them significantly → sharp money is pouring in on the underdog while public bets the favorite.
EV: If this shows +14%, betting that underdog has historically returned +14% per dollar wagered.
EXAMPLE: MONEYLINE PATTERN
Pattern: "Line: Slight dog (+110 to +200) | Move: Move toward (+2 to +5pts) | Tickets: 45-55% | Money: 55-65%"
Means: A slight underdog priced +110 to +200 where the line moved in their favor (they got cheaper to bet) while money split slightly toward them.
EV: Positive EV here = the market is undervaluing this team relative to where sharp money is pointing.
TICKET% VS MONEY%
Key insight: Ticket % = how many bettors. Money % = how much dollar value.
When money% >> ticket% → a few large bets (sharp money) → follow the money.
When ticket% >> money% → many small bets (public squares) → consider fading.
The pattern table shows you which combinations of these splits have historically produced positive EV.

LINE TRACKER MODEL — v2.0
A machine learning system that finds edges over sportsbooks by detecting when sharp money has moved a line and public books haven't caught up yet.
Every day at 3 PM ET, the model scrapes lines across 9 books (3 sharp, 6 public) via OddsJam, extracts 30+ movement features, runs them through a sport-specific XGBoost classifier, and outputs a ranked list of positive-EV bets sized for your bankroll. The pipeline runs fully automatically on GitHub's servers — no action needed from you.
⚠️
Edge numbers look huge right now — here's why
Until the model has 80+ real game outcomes per sport, it trains on synthetic data (injected noise around historical odds). That means edges like "36%" are an artifact of the bootstrap process — not real. The model will retrain on real outcomes automatically as games complete. Once a sport's AUC on the Backtest tab consistently exceeds 0.55, those edges are meaningful. Until then: treat LOW/CLV-only bets as informational, and focus on bets with multiple signal tags (SHARP, RLM, ARB).

Sport/Market
Which sport and bet type. h2h = moneyline (who wins outright). spreads = cover a points handicap. totals = over/under on combined score.
Side
The specific bet to place — team name plus the spread or total line. The number after the team is the handicap for spreads, or the game total for over/unders.
Odds
American-format price at the listed book at scrape time. Always verify the current price before placing — this number may be stale. If the current odds are worse than shown, re-check the edge.
Model%
The XGBoost classifier's estimated probability that this bet wins, after calibration. This is derived from how the line moved — not from team stats or injury reports.
Fair%
Pinnacle's no-vig (no margin) implied probability — the most accurate publicly-available estimate of the true odds. This is the benchmark the model competes against.
Edge
The core number. Model% minus Fair%. A +4.6% edge means the model believes this bet is underpriced by 4.6 percentage points. In early bootstrap, this number is inflated — see warning above.
EV%
Expected value as a percent of your stake. +4.1% means that for every $100 you bet, you'd expect to profit $4.10 over many bets. Positive EV is the whole goal.
Bet$
Kelly-sized bet for a $10,000 bankroll. Scale for your actual bankroll: if you have $5,000, bet half the listed amount; $2,000 bankroll → bet 20% of the listed amount.
Conf
HIGH = edge ≥ 7% and Kelly ≥ 2.5% — model's strongest tier. MEDIUM = edge ≥ 4% and Kelly ≥ 1.2% — solid bet. LOW = below those thresholds — smaller size or skip.
Signals
The reason(s) the model flagged this bet. The more signal tags shown, the more evidence stacked behind the pick. See the Signal Decoder below for what each one means.
📡 Signal Decoder

Signals tell you why the model likes the bet. More signals stacked = stronger conviction. A bet with only CLV_MODEL is the weakest; SHARP + RLM together is the strongest.

SHARP
Sharp Money Movement
Pinnacle's line moved significantly while public books stayed flat. This gap means smart money hit the sharp book but the public books haven't adjusted — that's your window to bet at the public book's stale price.
→ Strongest real-world signal. Prioritize when present.
RLM
Reverse Line Movement
65%+ of public tickets are on one side, but the line moved the other way. When the crowd is betting Team A but the line moves toward Team B, sharp bettors are hammering Team B and the books are responding to them — not the public.
→ Classic sharp-vs-public indicator. Strong when paired with SHARP.
FADE
Fade the Public
The public has heavily bet one side (80%+ of tickets) with no corresponding sharp action. Books haven't needed to move the line because sharp money hasn't confirmed it — fading the public crowd here has historically positive EV.
→ Weakest of the three; only use with edge above 3%.
ARB
Arbitrage Opportunity
Both sides of the same game are priced such that you can bet opposing outcomes across two books and guarantee a profit regardless of the result. The margin % shown is the guaranteed return. Requires accounts at both listed books and fast execution — arbs close within minutes.
→ Risk-free if executed correctly. Act immediately.
CLV
Model Edge Only (CLV)
The model's XGBoost output shows a positive edge vs Pinnacle's fair price, but no movement-based signal fired. This means the model detected a statistical pattern in the features but there's no independent confirmation from line movement or splits data.
→ Lowest conviction. Treat as informational during bootstrap.
⚕ RISK
Injury Risk — Bet Side Affected
A key player (QB, SP, PG, G, etc.) on the team you're betting is listed as Out or Doubtful on ESPN's injury report. The model's edge was calculated on historical data that may not reflect this roster change — the current line may already be adjusted, or may not be yet. Check the injury note for the specific player.
→ Reduce bet size or pass. Verify the line moved since the injury was announced.
⚕ OPP
Opponent Injured — Edge Booster
A key player on the opposing team is Out or Doubtful. This is a positive signal for the side you're betting — the opponent is weakened and the model's edge may be understated if the injury isn't fully priced in yet. Public books are often slow to adjust for opponent injuries.
→ Positive signal. Consider full-size or slight oversize within your Kelly limit.
🎯 Confidence Tiers & How to Size

The confidence tier combines edge size and Kelly fraction into a single actionable label. Use it to prioritize which bets to place when time or book limits are a constraint.

HIGH
Edge ≥ 7% · Kelly ≥ 2.5%
Bet the full listed Bet$ amount. These are the model's strongest outputs — place these first before the line moves.
MED
Edge ≥ 4% · Kelly ≥ 1.2%
Bet the listed amount or scale down to 50–75% if you want to be conservative. Good bets with solid backing.
LOW
Edge < 4% · Kelly < 1.2%
During bootstrap, skip these entirely — the edge is likely synthetic noise. Once AUC > 0.55, you can bet 25–50% of the listed amount on LOW bets with multiple signal tags.

Scaling for your bankroll: The Bet$ column assumes $10,000. Divide your actual bankroll by 10,000, then multiply: $5K bankroll → bet 50% of the listed amount. $2K → bet 20%.

Book selection: The Book column shows the best available price at scrape time. Always check the current price before placing — if the line has moved against you by more than 1–2 points (spreads) or 10+ cents (moneylines), recalculate whether the edge still exists.

✅ Your Daily Routine
1
Check this page after 3 PM ET
The pipeline runs at 3 PM ET every day. Hit ↺ Refresh in the header to load the latest slate. Games starting before 3 PM won't be on it — the model only outputs bets it has time to act on.
2
Filter for HIGH + MEDIUM confidence first
Sort or scan for HIGH bets, then MEDIUM. Skip LOW bets until the model has real outcome data (AUC > 0.55 on the Backtest tab). Look for bets with multiple signal tags — SHARP + RLM together is the strongest possible combination.
3
Cross-check injury reports
The model cannot see injury news. If a key player was announced as out within the last few hours, that's likely why the line moved — and the model will misread it as sharp action. Always verify on RotowWire or ESPN before placing.
4
Verify the current odds at the listed book
Open the listed sportsbook and confirm the current price. If the spread has moved from -3.5 to -4.5, or the moneyline has shifted by 20+ cents, the edge may be gone. For ARB signals, act within minutes — gaps close fast.
5
Size the bet using your bankroll ratio
Bet$ is sized for $10,000. Multiply the listed amount by (your bankroll ÷ 10,000). Never bet more than 4% of your bankroll on a single bet regardless of what the model says — that cap is built in, but verify it holds after scaling.
🚩 When to Skip a Bet

The model outputs every edge it finds — but not every edge is worth betting. Here are situations to skip even if the numbers look good.

✕
Edge > 15% with only CLV_MODEL signal
During bootstrap, edges above 15% with no movement signals are synthetic noise. The model hasn't seen enough real outcomes to trust its own probability estimates this much. Skip.
✕
Key injury announced in the last 2–4 hours
The model will read an injury-driven line move as sharp action. If you see a sudden line move and a star player is in the injury report, the "edge" is fake — the market is correcting for news, not inefficiency.
✕
Game starts in under 30 minutes
Lines are extremely settled close to tip-off/kickoff. Edges that appear in the final 30 minutes are usually stale data artifacts from the scraper — not real opportunities. Verify the live line manually before acting.
✕
Price at your book is already gone
If the current odds are worse than the model's listed odds by more than 5 cents (moneylines) or 0.5 points (spreads), recalculate the edge from scratch using the current price. A +4% edge at -110 can become 0% if the line moves to -120.
✕
CFB or CBB in the off-season
College markets outside of their regular season have thin action and abnormal line movement. The model's patterns don't apply. CFB: trust Sept–Jan only. CBB: Nov–Mar only.
⚙️ How the Model Works
1
Data Collection — OddsJam API
Every day at 3 PM ET, the pipeline pulls up to 365 days of historical odds plus today's live lines from OddsJam across 9 books and 6 sports (NFL, NBA, MLB, NHL, CFB, CBB). Each game gets a full timeline of price snapshots from opening line to close.
2
Feature Engineering — 30+ movement signals
For each game and bet side, 30+ features are calculated: how much Pinnacle moved (in full and in each time window), how fast it moved, whether public books followed or diverged, sharp vs public price gap, reverse line movement flag, number of directional reversals, time to game start, and more. These features capture the "story" of how the market moved.
3
XGBoost Classification — one model per sport per market
A separate XGBoost decision-tree classifier is trained for each combination of sport and market type (e.g. "NFL spreads", "NBA moneylines"). It learns which movement patterns historically resulted in a winning bet. Outputs are calibrated with Platt scaling to produce reliable probabilities.
4
Edge Detection — beat Pinnacle's no-vig price
The model's win probability is compared to Pinnacle's no-vig fair price (the market's sharpest estimate). If the model says 56% and Pinnacle's fair price implies 51%, that's a +5% edge. Any edge above the minimum threshold (currently 0.5%) is included on the slate.
5
Kelly Criterion Sizing — fractional, capped
Each bet is sized using the Kelly Criterion — a formula that maximizes long-run bankroll growth given the edge and odds. The model uses 25% of full Kelly (conservative) and caps individual bets at 4% of bankroll. Total portfolio exposure is capped at 20%.
6
Arbitrage Detection — cross-book scan
After generating the slate, the model scans all books for arbitrage: games where betting both sides (Team A at Book X, Team B at Book Y) guarantees a profit regardless of the outcome. When found, an ARB signal is added with the guaranteed margin percentage.
📊 Reading the Model's Health

The Backtest tab shows the model's current AUC score and bet history. Here's how to interpret what you see there.

AUC = 0.50
⚠ Bootstrap / Synthetic Data
The model is in early training. It's using artificially generated outcomes — predictions are not reliable yet. Normal when the pipeline is new.
AUC 0.51–0.54
⚠ Still building — real data accumulating
Some real game outcomes have been logged. The model is learning but needs more data. Treat HIGH bets with SHARP/RLM signals as tentative.
AUC ≥ 0.55
✓ Model is reliable for this sport
The model has seen enough real outcomes to make meaningful predictions. Edge percentages in the 3–10% range are credible. Time to raise MIN_EDGE_TO_BET to 2.5% in config.
CLV > 0
✓ Closing line value — long-run profitable
If bets are consistently beating the closing price (CLV > 0), the model is finding real edges before the market corrects. This is the gold standard for confirming the approach works.
All edges > 15%
⚠ Synthetic data artifact — not real edges
Normal at launch. Edges will compress to realistic 2–8% range as real outcome data accumulates and the model retrains. No action needed.
n_signals = 0
⚠ Only CLV signal — weakest bets
The movement signals (SHARP, RLM, FADE, ARB) haven't fired. The bet is based purely on the XGBoost model output with no corroborating evidence from line movement. Lowest priority.
🗄️ Data Sources
📡
OddsJam API — primary odds source
Pulls live and historical lines for all 9 books and 6 sports. Provides up to 365 days of historical movement data for training. Runs via GitHub Actions automatically — your API key in GitHub Secrets is the only credential needed.
🏦
Sharp Books: Pinnacle, Circa, Bookmaker
Used as the sharp reference price. Pinnacle sets the global standard for efficient pricing and is used as the CLV benchmark. Movement at these books is the core signal — they move first when sharp money hits.
🏪
Public Books: DraftKings, FanDuel, BetMGM, Bovada, William Hill, Bet365
Target books where you actually place bets. The model finds windows where these books are slow to adjust to sharp movement — that lag is where positive EV exists.
🤖
GitHub Actions — fully automated
The entire pipeline runs on GitHub's free servers every day at 3 PM ET. No local computer or server needed. Results are committed back to this repo automatically, and this dashboard updates within seconds of each run.
